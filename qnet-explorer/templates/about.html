About QNet Blockchain

The QNet Blockchain Project

QNet is a quantum-resistant blockchain platform built from the ground up to be secure, energy-efficient, and accessible to everyone, even on mobile devices.

Our Mission

We believe blockchain technology should be accessible to everyone, not just those with expensive hardware or technical knowledge. QNet was created to demonstrate that a single developer can build a fully functional, secure blockchain without massive resources.

Technical Foundations

  • Post-Quantum Cryptography: Uses Dilithium, a NIST approved PQC algorithm for quantum-resistant signatures
  • Efficient Consensus: Commit-reveal mechanism with external random beacon for energy efficiency
  • Mobile First: Optimized for low resource environments including smartphones
  • Modular Architecture: Core components in Rust, interface layers in Python
  • Decentralized Identity: Built-in DID support for Sybil resistance

Economic Model

QNet features a logarithmic emission curve that issues 90% of the total supply (2^32 coins) within the first 10 years. This creates a balanced approach between initial distribution to early adopters and long-term sustainability of the network.

Unique "Punk" Development Philosophy

QNet was created by a solo developer with help from AI tools, demonstrating that blockchain technology doesn't require large teams or VC funding. This "punk" approach to development echoes the original ethos of cryptocurrencies - technology built by individuals, for individuals, without corporate or institutional control.

Frequently Asked Questions

QNet uses Dilithium, a lattice-based cryptographic algorithm that has been selected by NIST as a standard for post-quantum digital signatures. This means that even quantum computers with significant qubits cannot break QNet's cryptographic security. Unlike traditional blockchains that rely on ECDSA signatures (vulnerable to Shor's algorithm running on quantum computers), QNet's foundation is built to withstand attacks from both classical and quantum adversaries.

The node adjusts its resource consumption based on battery status and network connectivity, ensuring that it doesn't drain your battery or consume excessive data. This is made possible through a combination of efficient data structures, pruned blockchain storage, and optimized consensus mechanisms that don't require energy-intensive computations.

The total supply is 2^32 (4,294,967,296) tokens. This quantum-based number was chosen deliberately, with 90% emitted over the first 10 years using a logarithmic curve rather than Bitcoin's halving model. This creates a more predictable emission schedule that balances initial distribution with long-term sustainability. The emission curve is designed to reward early participants while ensuring that new participants can still earn meaningful rewards in later years.

QNet employs a unique hardware fingerprinting system combined with Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) to ensure that each physical device can only run one node. This prevents malicious actors from spinning up multiple instances to gain control over the network. Additionally, the consensus mechanism includes reputation-based elements that make it costly to operate dishonestly, further discouraging Sybil attacks. This approach provides a more sustainable alternative to both Proof-of-Work's energy consumption and Proof-of-Stake's capital requirements.

QNet stands out through its combination of quantum resistance, mobile-first design, and solo developer creation. While most blockchains require specialized hardware, significant technical expertise, or large development teams, QNet aims to democratize blockchain participation. Its architecture is designed from first principles to be resilient against quantum attacks while remaining accessible to users with only smartphones. The project also embodies the original cypherpunk ethos of individual empowerment and decentralization, rather than corporate or venture-backed development.
